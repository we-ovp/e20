E20 Fuel

Clean & Efficient Energy

Explore how E20 fuel combines 20% ethanol with petrol for a sustainable future.

What is E20?

  • E20 fuel refers to a blend comprising 20% ethanol and 80% petrol. Ethanol is a renewable, bio-based fuel typically produced from crops like sugarcane, corn, and rice. It helps reduce dependence on fossil fuels, promotes energy security, and lowers greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Ethanol-blended fuels are emerging as a sustainable alternative to conventional fossil fuels. E20 fuel represents a major step in India’s journey towards cleaner energy, energy independence, and reduced carbon emissions. However, this shift also raises important questions about food versus fuel security — as crops once meant for food are now being used to produce fuel.

Ethanol and Combustion

Ethanol enhances fuel combustion by increasing the octane number(a measure of fuel’s resistance to premature detonation), improving engine knocking resistance, and promoting more stable combustion. Its oxygen-rich composition also reduces harmful emissions like carbon monoxide (CO) and hydrocarbons (HC).


Energy Efficiency

While ethanol benefits engine performance, it has a lower energy density than petrol. E20 fuel thus produces less energy per liter, leading to a slight reduction in mileage (2–7%), though modern E20-compatible engines can minimize this to around 1–2%.


Production of Ethanol

Ethanol is primarily produced through the fermentation of sugars and starches extracted from crops such as sugarcane, maize, and rice. The steps include:
  • Extraction of Sugars – from biomass or crops
  • Fermentation – microorganisms convert sugars to ethanol
  • Distillation – separates pure ethanol
  • Blending – mixed with petrol to create E20



    • Impact on vehicles

    Not all vehicles can use E20 fuel safely. E20-Compatible Vehicles: Newer models (post-April 2023) from brands like Hyundai and Maruti Suzuki are designed for E20. Older Vehicles: May face corrosion, engine wear, or fuel system damage due to ethanol’s moisture-absorbing and corrosive nature.


    Car Modifications Required

    Ethanol-resistant fuel hoses, filters, and injectors. ECU recalibration for correct air–fuel ratio. Flex-fuel sensors to adapt to variable ethanol content.


    Effect on Engine Life

    E20 does not harm E20-compliant engines. However, in older engines, ethanol may cause component corrosion or water absorption, shortening engine life if not designed for such blends.


    Sustainability of Ethanol Production

    Ethanol is renewable but its sustainability depends on: 1. Type of feedstock used (sugarcane is better than maize) 2. Water and land usage 3. Efficiency of conversion technologies Overuse of food crops for fuel can create pressure on food security — leading to the “Food vs Fuel” debate.


    Impact on Fuel Imports

    Ethanol blending reduces India’s dependence on imported crude oil. Locally-produced ethanol strengthens energy security, supports farmers’ incomes, and reduces vulnerability to global oil price fluctuations.


    Price Comparison

    Despite being bio-based, E20 fuel is not cheaper than regular petrol due to higher procurement and production costs. Current pricing policies prioritize energy security and emission reduction over consumer cost savings.
